Spicy Smoky Party Jollof Rice

A spicy and delicious smoky party jollof perfect for a typical Nigerian party settings

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 25 people

Difficulty Level: Beginner

Ingredients:

  • 3kg rice grains
  • 3 cups beaf/chicken stock
  • 600g plantain
  • 500g onions
  • vegetable oil
  • salt to taste
  • 15-16 seasoning cubes (preferable knor)
  • 1kg red ball pepper
  • 6-7 scotch bonnet pepper (atarodo)
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1kg fresh or chopped tomatoes
  • 100g Butter/margarine
  • 2 teaspoon curry
  • 2 teaspoon thyme
  • 6 pieces bay leaves

Instructions:

  1. The first step is to season and cook the meat until tender (with salt, seasoning cubes, curry & thyme, and sliced onions)
  2. Extract the stock and keep it for later use(Water extracted from the meat)
  3. Blend the tomatoes, Redball pepper(tatashe), Scotch bonnet pepper (Atarodo), garlic cloves, and onions.
  4. Turn the blended ingredients in a pot bring them to a boil until the water is completely dry.
  5. In another cooking pot add the vegetable oil, add sliced onion and allow it to saute for about 2 minutes, then go ahead to add the tomato paste and fry for about 3 minutes.
  6. Add the boiled blended pepper, stir vigorously.
  7. Now start adding the seasonings: Curry, thyme, seasoning cubes/powder, salt and the chicken or meat stock earlier extracted. You may add more water 2- 3 cups of water that would be enough to cook the rice. (Do not add too much water as we donâ€™t want soggy jollof rice) Cover and allow everything to get incorporated for about 5 minutes.
  8. Go ahead and start adding the washed rice(Rice should be washed about 5-6 times or until the water is clear) Then stir the rice in the sauce very well, add bay leaves and butter/ Margarine. Cover with foil first before covering with the pot lid.
  9. Cook on low heat, the foil steams the rice and cooks it. Check after the first 30 minutes, taste and adjust the seasoning if the need be. Then cover again
  10. Check again after 15- 20 minutes to see if the jollof rice is well cooked. (The overall cooking time varies on the type of rice used.)
  11. Use your turning stick to stir the rice through the bottom and evenly.
  12. Then cover only with the lid again for about 4 minutes and this time increase the heat to the highest to allow the rice to burn a little bit to give that smoky effect.
  13. Turn off the heat and stir evenly again through the bottom. Your smoky party Jollof rice is ready to be served. Serve with beef turkey or chicken.
